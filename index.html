Dad of all Magazines: RIP Magazine

Posted by Kusuma and vijaya on Monday, Aug 23 2003

A magazine is a periodical publication, generally published on a regular schedule(often weekly or monthly), containing a variety of content. They are generally financed by advertising, purchase price, prepaid subscriptions,or by a combination of the three.

In the technical sense a journal has continuous pagination throughout a volume. Thus, Bloomberg Businessweek, which starts each issue anew with page one, is a magazine, but the Journal of Business Communication, which continues the same sequence of pagination throughout the coterminous year, is a journal.

What is RIP?

RIP stands for RestIn Peace. Some professional or trade publications are also peer-reviewed, for example the Journal of Accountancy. Non-peer-reviewed academic or professional publications are generally professional magazines.

The word "magazine" derives from Arabic makhazin )مخازن the broken plural of makhzan مخزن( meaning "depot, storehouse" (originally military storehouse); that comes to English via Middle French magasin and Italian magazzino. In its original sense, the word "magazine" referred to a storage space or device. In the case of written publication, it refers to a collection of written articles.
